I'm a PhD candidate at State University, specializing in low-power embedded systems and machine learning inference at the edge. My work bridges the gap between theoretical algorithms and real-world hardware constraints.

Before academia, I spent three years in industry building high-reliability firmware for aerospace applications, which taught me that engineering is ultimately about delivering systems people can trust.

When I'm not in the lab, I mentor undergrad engineering students and contribute to open-source toolchains for RISC-V development.
